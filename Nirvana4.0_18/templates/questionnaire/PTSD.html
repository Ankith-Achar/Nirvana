PTSD Assessment

This questionnaire is designed to help assess symptoms commonly associated with Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD). Please answer each question honestly, considering how you've felt over the past month.

Rate each item on a scale from 0 (Not at all) to 5 (Extremely/Almost always).

Important: This is not a diagnostic tool. If you're experiencing distress, please consult a mental health professional.

1. Had nightmares about the event(s) or thought about the event(s) when you did not want to?

2. Tried hard not to think about the event(s) or went out of your way to avoid situations that reminded you of the event(s)?

3. Been constantly on guard, watchful, or easily startled?

4. Felt numb or detached from people, activities, or your surroundings?

5. Felt guilty or unable to stop blaming yourself or others for the event(s) or any problems the event(s) may have caused?

6. How often do you avoid or delay getting started on a task that requires a lot of thought?

7. How often do you have difficulty unwinding and relaxing when you have time to yourself?

8. How often do you feel restless or fidgety?

9. How often are you distracted by activity or noise around you?

10. How often do you have difficulty concentrating on what people say to you, even when they are speaking to you directly?

11. How often do you feel overly active and compelled to do things, like you were driven by a motor?

12. How often do you have difficulty sleeping or staying asleep because of stress or worry?

13. How often do you feel distant or cut off from others?

14. How often do you blame yourself for things that happened in the past?

15. How often do you relive past upsetting experiences in your mind?
